---
title: "Challenges"
description: "Create challenge templates and accept multi-day tracks to build long-term habits."
---

Challenges are multi-day commitments you make to yourself. You first create a **challenge template** (a reusable blueprint), then **accept** it to start an active run. While active you can log daily progress notes, and when you're done you either complete or abandon it.

## Concepts

### Challenge template

A template is the blueprint for a challenge. It is not tied to any specific run — multiple users could accept the same template.

```typescript
export interface ChallengeTemplate {
  id: number;
  title: string;
  description: string;
  duration_days: number;
  created_at: string;
  updated_at: string;
}
```

### User challenge

A user challenge is created when you accept a template. It tracks your personal run: status, start date, completion date, and your diary notes.

```typescript
export interface UserChallenge {
  id: number;
  user_id: string;
  template_id: number;
  status: 'ACTIVE' | 'COMPLETED' | 'ABANDONED';
  start_date: string;
  completed_at: string | null;
  notes?: string | null;
  created_at: string;
  updated_at: string;
  template?: ChallengeTemplate;
}
```

### Status lifecycle

A challenge always starts as `ACTIVE` and transitions to one of two terminal states:

```
ACTIVE → COMPLETED
ACTIVE → ABANDONED
```

Challenges in `COMPLETED` or `ABANDONED` status appear in your history but can no longer be modified.

## Browsing the catalog

The Challenges page lists the available templates in a dropdown when you open the "Novo Desafio" modal. Each entry shows the template title and its duration in days. The catalog holds up to 10 templates at a time.

## Creating a challenge template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the modal">
    On the Challenges page, click **Novo Desafio**. The modal opens on the "Aceitar novo Desafio" view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to create view">
    Click **Criar Desafio** at the bottom of the modal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the fields">
    | Field | Requirement |
    |---|---|
    | **Título** | Minimum 3 characters |
    | **Descrição** | Minimum 10 characters |
    | **Duração (Dias)** | Between 1 and 365 |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Salvar novo Desafio**. The new template appears in the catalog immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The catalog has a hard limit of 10 templates. If you have reached the limit, the create button is replaced by a notice and a **Excluir um desafio** option to remove an existing template before creating a new one.
</Warning>

## Accepting a challenge

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the modal">
    Click **Novo Desafio** on the Challenges page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a template">
    Choose a template from the **Modelo do Desafio** dropdown. The dropdown shows each template's title and duration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept">
    Click **Aceitar Desafio**. Your active challenge is set immediately and the page reloads to show the active challenge card.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only one challenge can be active at a time. If you already have an `ACTIVE` challenge, the API will reject a new accept request. You must complete or abandon your current challenge before starting a new one.
</Warning>

## Managing your active challenge

When a challenge is active, the **Desafio Ativo** section on the Challenges page shows a full management card with:

- **Title and description** from the template
- **Start date** and a day counter (e.g., "Dia 4 de 30")
- **Challenge diary** — a log of all your progress notes
- **Actions** — complete or abandon buttons

### Logging progress (diary)

The challenge diary (`Diário do Desafio`) lets you record a text note each day to document your progress.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write a note">
    Type your progress update in the text area (max 500 characters).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the entry">
    Click **Registrar Progresso**. The entry is timestamped and prepended to the diary log.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Notes are stored as a JSON array on the `notes` field of your `UserChallenge` record. Previous notes are displayed in reverse-chronological order (newest first) above the input.

### Completing a challenge

Click **Completar Desafio** to mark the challenge as `COMPLETED`. This action is permanent — the challenge moves to your history and cannot be resumed.

### Abandoning a challenge

Click **Abandonar** to open a confirmation modal. Confirm to set the status to `ABANDONED`. Like completion, this is irreversible — you cannot resume progress from an abandoned challenge.

## Challenge history

The **Histórico** section lists all your past challenges with a `COMPLETED` or `ABANDONED` status. If you have no history yet, an empty state is shown.
